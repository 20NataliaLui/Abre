The Pacific: A.D. 601 to 700

THE PACIFIC

Easter Island craftsmen began building altars well before 690 and there is some evidence that prototypic, naturalistic statues may have been placed on them at that time. Although true Polynesians may well have been in Hawaii and possibly the Marquesas in this century, they had not yet migrated as far south as Easter Island and New Zealand. (Ref. 1)
